## How to Read This File

Each entry shows:
- **Price action**: Open → High / Low → Close, % change, ORB range
- **ORB trade**: direction, R2 P&L, exit type
- **Narrative**: what drove the day and what it meant for the strategy

Exit codes: `TARGET` = 2R target hit | `STOP` = ORB-edge stop hit | `EOD` = held to 15:59

### Key Regime Observations from 2026

1. **Anthropic shock (Feb 3-4)** produced the two best trades of the year — both short ORBs hitting 2R targets on consecutive days. Sustained macro narratives drive multi-day ORB momentum.

2. **Geopolitical whipsaw (March 9-11)** produced four consecutive ORB stop-outs. When the market is reacting to binary headline risk (war/peace), the ORB encodes noise rather than price discovery.

3. **Trump tariff shock (Jan 20)** produced a stop-out long ORB followed the next day by the month's best trade — a target-hit long on the relief rally. Gap-down opens on geopolitical news often trap the contrarian direction; the *next* day's clean ORB is the better trade.

4. **Narrow ORB + trending day** remained the cleanest setup: Jan 6, Jan 15-16, Feb 12, Feb 26, Mar 12 all featured narrow ORBs (< $2.20) preceding clean directional trends.

5. **Wide ORB days (> $5.00)** — Feb 3, Feb 5, Feb 17, Feb 20, Mar 2 — produced inconsistent results even when direction was correct, confirming the Q4 (Wide ORB) quartile finding from the full dataset (PF 1.04 vs. Q2's 1.37).
